Onboarding: Customer deployment bundle

How this works
• Once your application is approved, you download a ready-made deployment bundle.
• You deploy it into your own AWS account using Terraform.
• After that, the platform runs automatically on the schedule you chose and saves reports to your S3 bucket.
Customer-owned deployment Terraform provisioned Runner + optimisers HTML + JSON reports Scheduled with EventBridge

This page shows the customer deployment process and what is included in your generated bundle.

Deployment steps

Expected time: around 10 to 20 minutes if you already have AWS access to deploy the required resources.

  • 1

    Complete your application and onboarding details.

    You choose your AWS region, reporting schedule and the services you want the platform to check.
  • 2

    Download your generated customer deployment bundle.

    The bundle includes everything you need to deploy the platform in your AWS account.
  • 3

    Check the configuration files.

    Make sure the AWS region, schedule, report bucket and selected services are correct before you deploy.
  • 4

    Deploy the stack with Terraform.

    Terraform creates the Lambdas, IAM role, schedule, log groups and report bucket in your account.
  • 5

    Let the platform run automatically.

    Each scheduled run checks your selected services, estimates savings and uploads reports to your S3 bucket under reports/<customer>/<run_id>.

What gets deployed

  • One runner Lambda that coordinates the checks
  • Only the optimiser Lambdas you selected during onboarding
  • IAM execution role and permissions
  • CloudWatch log groups
  • EventBridge schedule
  • S3 bucket for saved reports

Supported optimisation engines

Amazon S3 Checks for cheaper storage options for data that is accessed less often.
Amazon RDS Looks for database instances that may be larger than they need to be.
Amazon ECS Finds container services that may be using more capacity than needed.
Amazon EKS Checks whether Kubernetes node capacity can be reduced safely.
EC2 Spot Instances Highlights workloads that may be suitable for lower-cost Spot pricing.

What is in your download

  • Terraform files to deploy the platform
  • The runner and selected optimiser packages
  • Your customer configuration
  • Simple deployment and troubleshooting guides

Deploy commands

cd customer-deployment/terraform terraform init terraform apply

Your main settings are already filled in for you, so in most cases you only need to review them and deploy.

Runner workflow

  • EventBridge starts the runner Lambda on your chosen schedule.
  • The runner checks which services are enabled for your account.
  • Each selected optimiser analyses its service and estimates possible savings.
  • The runner combines the results into one report.
  • Reports are uploaded to S3 as HTML and JSON files.
Report output
• An HTML report that is easy to read.
• A JSON report for automation or further analysis.
• Both are stored in S3 under reports/<customer>/<run_id>.html and reports/<customer>/<run_id>.json.
