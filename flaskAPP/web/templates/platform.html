Deployment workflow

Approved customers receive a Terraform bundle containing the runner Lambda, enabled optimiser Lambdas, IAM policy, EventBridge schedule and reporting configuration. The generated package reflects the selected region, services, report frequency and delivery settings already stored during onboarding.

What the customer actually gets

A direct deployment path, a report bucket, automated execution and email notifications without requiring ongoing manual analysis by the platform operator.

Why this feels operational

A first report can run immediately after deployment, then continue through EventBridge scheduling. That gives the customer something actionable from day one rather than waiting for a later manual setup step.

Supported AWS services

The current platform supports Amazon S3, Amazon RDS, Amazon ECS, Amazon EKS and EC2 Spot opportunity analysis.

Consistent report structure

Each optimiser follows the same reporting shape so cost baselines, recommendations and savings can be reviewed in one consolidated output.

Designed for maintainability

Onboarding, deployment, optimisation and reporting are separated cleanly so the system can evolve without turning into one tightly coupled Lambda package.

Why this problem grows quietly

Cloud usage expands through retained data, oversized compute and always-on workloads. Costs usually drift before they fail, which is why regular optimisation review matters.

Why keep the graph here

Global data growth helps explain why storage and supporting services become more expensive over time. In practice, that shows up as retained objects, overprovisioned databases, oversized container platforms and missed Spot opportunities.

Covered services

The platform currently scans five AWS services and returns recommendations in one consistent reporting structure.

Amazon S3

Inactivity, storage-class opportunities and retained data that may be costing money without ongoing value.

Amazon RDS

Managed database overprovisioning and rightsizing opportunities where performance risk appears low.

Amazon ECS

Low-utilisation container services and oversized CPU or memory reservations that create wasted capacity.

Amazon EKS

Cluster capacity drift, nodegroup sizing signals and opportunities to reduce overprovisioned compute.

EC2 Spot Instances

Interruption-tolerant workloads that may be suitable for lower-cost Spot capacity instead of full on-demand pricing.
