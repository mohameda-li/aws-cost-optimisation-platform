Why cloud optimisation matters

Cloud waste is rarely a single big mistake — it’s the accumulation of small inefficiencies: data kept beyond its value, capacity left running “just in case” and workloads sized for peaks that rarely happen. By increasing visibility and continuously checking usage signals, the platform highlights the highest-impact changes first and estimates what you could save each month, so teams can reduce cost without compromising reliability.

What you could save

Most AWS waste comes from services running when they are not needed, storage kept long past its value, and workloads that are larger than required. This platform flags the most common inefficiencies and emails you a report with estimated monthly savings.

Storage tiering (S3)

Identify buckets with low access patterns and recommend moving data to cheaper tiers. Typical savings are 10 to 60% on the affected storage, depending on access frequency.

Oversized databases (RDS)

Detect underutilised instances and propose smaller instance classes where safe. Typical savings are 20 to 50% on the affected database spend.

Idle containers (ECS)

Find services with consistently low CPU or memory usage and recommend rightsizing. Typical savings are 15 to 40% on the affected workloads.

Cluster efficiency (EKS)

Highlight inefficient node utilisation signals and opportunities to reduce capacity. Typical savings are 10 to 35% where clusters are overprovisioned.

Spot usage (EC2 Spot Instances)

Recommend using Spot capacity for suitable workloads to cut compute costs. Spot can reduce eligible compute spend by up to 70 to 90%, depending on workload tolerance.

What you receive

You receive an email report that lists recommendations by priority, shows the estimated monthly savings, and explains what to change. If no issues are found, you will receive a short confirmation summary.
